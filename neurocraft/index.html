🦢 origami beginner friendly

transform a flat square of paper into something magical — no scissors, no glue, just folds. origami is deeply meditative and great for focus. each fold is a small satisfying victory 🧠✨

  • we start with simple models (cranes, frogs, hearts) and level up from there
  • no pressure to finish — half-folded cranes are still cute
  • super fidget-friendly: the repetitive folding is lowkey therapeutic
  • paper provided, but bring your faves if you have them!
📦 what we provide: origami paper in various colors and sizes, instruction sheets, diagrams for multiple difficulty levels
← back to workshops

📄 papercrafts beginner friendly

from tiny notebooks to pop-up cards, paper can do SO much. this is a low-mess, high-reward craft that lets you make something cute and functional. 🎁

  • mini bookbinding, envelopes, cards, tiny boxes, paper flowers
  • mix with stamps, washi tape, stickers for extra cuteness
  • perfect if you like seeing tangible results quickly
  • great for people who love stationery (you know who you are)
📦 what we provide: cardstock, colored paper, scissors, rulers, bone folders, washi tape, stickers, stamps
← back to workshops

🧵 weaving medium

there's something incredibly satisfying about the over-under rhythm of weaving. it's repetitive in the best way — almost like a physical fidget, but you end up with something gorgeous. 🌈

  • we use simple cardboard looms or small frame looms — no big equipment needed
  • experiment with colors, textures, and patterns
  • the pattern-making aspect is great for pattern-loving brains
  • wall hangings, coasters, mini tapestries — your call
📦 what we provide: small frame looms, yarn in various colors and textures, weaving needles, scissors
← back to workshops

🪡 crochet all levels

crochet is one of those skills that feels impossible at first and then suddenly CLICKS. the repetitive motion is really soothing and it's extremely portable. 🧸

  • complete beginners welcome — we'll teach the basic stitch from scratch
  • experienced crocheters can bring their WIPs (works in progress)
  • make granny squares, amigurumi, dishcloths, or just vibe with the motion
  • yarn and hooks provided — bring your own if you have a fave
  • ask for help any time — the group loves a yarn emergency
📦 what we provide: yarn in various weights and colors, crochet hooks (multiple sizes), stitch markers, beginner pattern cards
← back to workshops

🫧 air clay beginner friendly

air-dry clay is incredibly satisfying to squish, shape, and transform. it's mess-friendly, no kiln required, and dries overnight. extremely good for hands that need something to do. 🏺

  • make tiny sculptures, pendants, charms, miniature food, abstract shapes
  • texture it with stamps, lace, leaves — anything goes
  • paint it once it dries at home (bring it to the next session!)
  • satisfying tactile experience even if you "just" squish it for an hour
📦 what we provide: air-dry clay (white + colored), clay tools, rolling pins, stamps, texture plates, zip bags to take your work home
← back to workshops

✂️ collage all levels

cutting up magazines and pasting together new worlds is one of the most freeing art forms. no drawing skills required. your aesthetic instincts are enough. 🎭

  • analog collage: scissors + glue + magazine mountains
  • make mood boards, postcards, art journal pages, posters
  • great for non-linear thinkers — you can let your eye lead
  • deeply cathartic if you want to shred something (the magazines)
  • bring your own printed photos or mementos to incorporate!
📦 what we provide: stacks of old magazines, newspapers, tissue paper, cardstock, scissors, glue sticks, mod podge, markers
← back to workshops

📰 zine making all levels

a zine is a self-published mini magazine — totally yours to fill with whatever you want. rants, comics, poetry, recipes, lists, drawings, nothing at all. it's the most punk craft. ✊📓

  • we'll show you the classic one-sheet 8-page fold (no staples needed)
  • no rules about content — express literally anything
  • mix drawing, collage, writing, stamps, whatever vibes
  • swap zines with other attendees if you want!
  • great for people who have lots of Thoughts and Feelings
📦 what we provide: paper, scissors, markers, pens, stamps, collage supplies, folding guides, staplers

🌟 weekly craft hangout — neurodivergent creative club recurring

what it is: a guilt-free weekly crafting session in Berlin for neurodivergent folks + friends. no pressure to finish anything, ever.

the vibe: come as you are. bring your current project, start a new one, or just show up and see what happens. ADHD-friendly pacing, sensory-considerate space, zero judgment.

what's available: paint, yarn, crochet hooks, beads, coloring books, origami paper, markers, and more. feel free to bring your own supplies or current WIPs!

💰 donation: minimum 4–5€ to cover materials and the space. paypal: [email protected]

📸 follow us: @neurocrafting.berlin (ﾉ◕ヮ◕)ﾉ*:･ﾟ✧

🦢 origami evening workshop

an evening dedicated to the art of folding! from beginner cranes to more complex models. all paper and instruction sheets provided. beginner-friendly, experienced folders also welcome.

🪡 crochet + craft social workshop

bring your WIP or start something new! hooks and yarn provided for beginners. a cozy, chatty evening of yarn and vibes. perfect for anxious hands that need something to do.

✂️ collage + zine night workshop

mountains of magazines, scissors, glue, and your imagination. make a collage or fold your own 8-page zine. swap with other attendees at the end if you want 💌

🫧 air clay sculpting session workshop

squish, shape, and sculpt with air-dry clay. make charms, tiny sculptures, or just enjoy the tactile experience. clay dries overnight at home — paint it and bring it back to show us!

neurodivergent artist & maker based in Berlin. i started the NCC because i wanted a place where people like me could create without the pressure of finishing things or being "good" at them.

i'm into collage, zine-making, photography, and starting approximately 47 projects i'll never finish — and being very okay with that.

i believe creativity doesn't have to look productive to be meaningful. sometimes making a weird little paper frog is enough. 🐸
